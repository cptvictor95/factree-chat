# factree.fm — Logo prompt for Nano Banana Pro

Use this in [Nano Banana Pro](https://nanobananapro.com/) (or similar AI logo tools) to generate logo concepts. The prompt is based on the research brief in `research-brief-factree-fm-naming-brand-seo-logo.md`.

## Copy-paste prompt (main)

```
Logo for "factree.fm", a synchronized music listening room web app where friends hear the same second of the same YouTube video in one shared queue with chat. Wordmark-first: the text "factree.fm" is the hero, with distinctive modern typography—warm and minimal, not childish or corporate. Optional small icon suggesting togetherness or sync: aligned shapes, shared wave, or overlapping forms (no music notes, headphones, tree, or radio). Style: flat vector, SVG-style, clean geometric sans or warm modern serif, memorable and ownable. Color palette: warm charcoal or dark slate, soft amber or warm orange accent, white or off-white background. Must work in single color and at small sizes (favicon, app icon). --no realistic photo, shading, complex details, musical notation, literal tree, headphones, vinyl
```

## Nano Banana Pro tips (from research)

- **Text in logo:** Put the exact text you want in **double quotes** (`"factree.fm"`).
- **Clean output:** Use `flat vector`, `SVG style`, `white background` and `--no realistic photo, shading, complex details` for web-ready logos.
- **Refinement:** If the first result is too literal (e.g. music notes), add to the negative part: `--no musical notation, headphones, vinyl, tree silhouette`.
- **Export:** Prefer high-resolution and transparent background for app icon and favicon. For social previews, export a **1200×630** version as `og-image.png` and place it in `public/` so it is served at `https://factree.fm/og-image.png` (used by Open Graph and Twitter cards).

---

## What to avoid (from research)

- Music clichés: notes, clefs, headphones, vinyl, discs.
- Literal “tree” imagery unless highly stylized and integrated.
- Radio/FM literals: antenna, frequency waves (unless deliberately retro).
- Busy or decorative details that don’t scale to favicon size.
